# Planning Direct Mail with Canada Post: What to Decide Before Printing

Direct mail works best when the format, audience and message are planned before printing.

A postcard, flyer or brochure should not be designed in isolation and then forced into a mailing process afterward.

## Choose the audience first

Neighbourhood mail, targeted options and addressed mail all serve different campaign goals. The audience decision should come before final design.

## Pick the format around the message

A postcard is strong for a simple offer. A flyer gives more room. A folded brochure can explain several services. A door hanger can support local hand delivery around job sites or service areas.

## Give the customer one next step

Ask the customer to call, visit a landing page, scan a QR code, request a quote or use an offer code. Do not ask the piece to do everything at once.

## Confirm requirements before printing

Final size, address area, quantity, stock, bundling, delivery timing and Canada Post requirements should be reviewed before the full order is produced.
